Key Stage 1 (Years 1 & 2) National Curriculum

A clean, year-by-year breakdown of the KS1 National Curriculum for English, Maths, and Science in England.

🇬🇧 English (Year 1 & 2)

The KS1 English curriculum focuses on building strong foundations in Spoken Language, Reading, and Writing.

Year 1

AreaKey Learning Objectives
Spoken LanguageListen and respond appropriately to adults and peers; ask and answer questions; maintain attention and participate in collaborative conversations.
Word ReadingApply phonic knowledge to decode words; read common exception words; read words with contractions.
Reading ComprehensionDevelop pleasure in reading and motivation to read; understand books by drawing on what they already know.
Writing (Transcription)Spell words containing each of the 40+ phonemes taught; spell common exception words; spell the days of the week.
Writing (Composition)Write sentences by saying out loud what they are going to write about; sequence sentences to form short narratives.
Grammar & VocabularyLeave spaces between words; begin to use capital letters, full stops, question marks, and exclamation marks.

Year 2

AreaKey Learning Objectives
Spoken LanguageParticipate in discussions, presentations, performances, role-play, and improvisations; articulate and justify answers.
Word ReadingRead accurately most words of two or more syllables; read most common exception words; read aloud with expression.
Reading ComprehensionDevelop pleasure in reading; discuss the sequence of events in books and how items of information are related.
Writing (Transcription)Spell most common exception words; spell words with suffixes; use an apostrophe for contraction and possession (singular).
Writing (Composition)Write narratives about personal experiences and those of others; write poetry and non-fiction; evaluate and edit their writing.
Grammar & VocabularyUse expanded noun phrases to describe and specify; use subordination and coordination; learn how to use punctuation.

➗ Mathematics (Year 1 & 2)

The KS1 maths curriculum is designed to ensure pupils develop a secure foundation of mathematical knowledge.

Year 1

AreaKey Learning Objectives
Number & Place ValueCount to and across 100, forwards and backwards; count in multiples of 2, 5, and 10; identify one more and one less.
Addition & SubtractionRepresent and use number bonds and related subtraction facts within 20; solve one-step problems.
Multiplication & DivisionSolve one-step problems involving multiplication and division (using concrete objects and arrays).
FractionsRecognise, find, and name a half as one of two equal parts; recognise, find, and name a quarter.
MeasurementCompare, describe, and solve practical problems for lengths, mass, capacity, and time; recognise denominations of coins.
GeometryRecognise and name common 2-D and 3-D shapes; describe position, direction, and movement.

Year 2

AreaKey Learning Objectives
Number & Place ValueCount in steps of 2, 3, and 5 from 0, and in tens from any number; recognise the place value of each digit in a two-digit number.
Addition & SubtractionRecall and use addition and subtraction facts to 20 fluently; add and subtract numbers using concrete objects and mentally.
Multiplication & DivisionRecall and use multiplication and division facts for the 2, 5, and 10 times tables; recognise odd and even numbers.
FractionsRecognise, find, name, and write fractions 1/3, 1/4, 2/4, and 3/4; recognise the equivalence of 2/4 and 1/2.
MeasurementChoose and use appropriate standard units to estimate and measure; tell and write the time to five minutes.
GeometryIdentify and describe the properties of 2-D and 3-D shapes; compare and sort common 2-D and 3-D shapes.

🔬 Science (Year 1 & 2)

The KS1 science curriculum encourages pupils to be curious and ask questions about the world around them.

Year 1

AreaKey Learning Objectives
Working ScientificallyAsk simple questions; perform simple tests; observe closely using simple equipment; identify and classify.
PlantsIdentify and name a variety of common wild and garden plants, including deciduous and evergreen trees.
Animals, Including HumansIdentify and name a variety of common animals, including fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals.
Everyday MaterialsDistinguish between an object and the material from which it is made; describe the simple physical properties.
Seasonal ChangesObserve changes across the four seasons; observe and describe weather associated with the seasons.

Year 2

AreaKey Learning Objectives
Working ScientificallyAsk simple questions; perform simple tests; observe closely; identify and classify; use observations to suggest answers.
Living Things & Their HabitatsExplore and compare differences between living, dead, and things that have never been alive; describe different habitats.
PlantsObserve and describe how seeds and bulbs grow into mature plants; find out how plants need water, light, and temperature.
Animals, Including HumansNotice that animals have offspring which grow into adults; describe the importance of exercise, eating right, and hygiene.
Uses of Everyday MaterialsIdentify and compare the suitability of everyday materials; find out how shapes of solid objects can be changed.
